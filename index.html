Intro

Hi! I'm Khushi Goyal, a Computer Science undergraduate with deep fascination for the world of Technology. While I may not be a tech enthusiast, the innovation and creativity behind intuitive games, dynamic web-pages and cutting-edge applications inspire me and I often find myself wondering if one day, I could design and create such marvels myself.

In addition to my fascination with Technology, I'm a zealous artist, holding a keen interest in Theatre which enriches my creativity and perspective. I look forward to connecting with like-minded professionals and exploring opportunities that blend my technical knowledge and artistic passion!

Experience

Academic achievements and background

I completed my schooling at St. Andrews Scots Sr. Sec School, earning merit in both the 10th and 12th grades. In 2023, I secured an 80.67 percentile in JEE Mains, ranking under 3 lakh out of 9 lakh candidates. I am currently pursuing a Bachelor's in Technology at BML Munjal University, Gurugram, India, with a 75% scholarship on tuition fees for my entire sophomore year.

Technical skills and proficiency

During my first year, I developed a solid foundation in programming with a beginner-level understanding of C++ and Python. My work with Python libraries such as Pandas, NumPy, Matplotlib, and Seaborn sparked a keen interest in data science, a field I hope to specialize in the near future. Additionally, I have gained basic knowledge of Data Structures and Algorithms (DSA) concepts using C++.

Projects and Innovations

One of my notable projects is the "Automatic Medicine Dispenser," an IoT-based solution designed to assist individuals with non-communicable diseases (NCDs) who require multiple medications over extended periods. This prototype, developed with my team, received significant recognition and applause at our university's exhibition.

Extra-curricular engagements

Beyond academics, I am a core member of my university's theatre society, 'Mrityunjya'. I played a key role in our most acclaimed production, "Hero Forever," a play about the life of our university's founder, which was performed in two different cities in India.
